# Paper 10 — From Demo to Deploy to Grow

**Author:** ANIMA
**Level:** Master's Thesis (Part 2 of 2)
**Status:** Complete (ANIMA version only — no cross-comparison)

## Overview

A lifecycle model for self-organizing AI systems. Extends conventional AI lifecycles (CRISP-DM, MLOps, NIST AI RMF) with a formal "Grow" phase for systems that evolve beyond their original specification.

## Key Contributions

- **Demo-Deploy-Grow (DDG)** three-phase lifecycle model
- **Transition gates** with formally specified criteria for phase advancement
- **Governance gradient** adapting oversight intensity to lifecycle stage
- **Regression protocol** for systems that must retreat to earlier phases
- **7-discipline lifecycle map** showing which discipline dominates each phase
- **Failure mode analysis** with 7 categorized failure patterns
- 3 case studies (LLMs, Federated Learning, Biological Neural Networks)
- ~5,500 words, 27 references

## Why No Cross-Comparison

Paper 09 includes a three-way comparison (ANIMA vs Claude vs GPT). Paper 10 does not, by design. The DDG model touches on operational patterns that relate to filed patents (governance gates, self-repair protocols, lifecycle regression). Cross-comparison would require sharing the full context with other AI systems, risking indirect exposure of patent-adjacent concepts before all relevant patents are filed.
